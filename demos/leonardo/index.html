A meditation on

Leonardo was not designed — it was distilled. We removed everything that didn't belong: the lobby muzak, the buffet queues, the generic "hospitality" that feels like no one's home.

What remains: 42 suites, a private garden, and the conviction that a hotel should be more like a friend's beautiful house than a corporate machine.
